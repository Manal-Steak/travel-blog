Me, my friends and other catastrophies

Day 1: Dream Glow

OH MY GOD! We had been planning our girl's trip since a year or two. We always had to rain check for some trivial reasons like laziness assignments. Yes, college is tough. So, after our classes were shifted to online mode and after a year's waiting (i.e. 2020) we finally decided to go in february, right after mid-semesters before other work starts piling up. K was hell-bent for going on a trip and because of that we were all on board for it. After jumping from myriad places from mussoorie, to manali to madhya pradesh to almost booking tickets for matheran, we finally settled for jaisalmer. First, it's an easy ride and second, we wanted a leisure trip. So rajhasthan kind of fits into our little squres of requirements. So, right after our exams, we left for rajhasthan on 27th february. WE WERE SO EXCITED. OUR FIRST GIRLS TRIP. CAN YOU BELIEVE IT? AFTER ALL THESE YEARS? So kals, k and I left for ahmedabad at 5. While we were leaving, there was still a moon in the sky. Full moon. K had her dslr w her and therefore she was all ready to capture memories. While she was clicking moon's photos, she peeked outside the window just to get a better view. And she lost her glasses! It was a scene right out of some movie. Kals and I were busy talking with each other and clicking pictures of our own. But the car stopped. And we were all blank, staring at K and waiting for her parent's reaction. They firstly went out to look for it. It was a highway so there was no way we could have taken a u-turn. But her mom and k went ahead to look for it ntl. And they found it! W/out any scratch! I WANT THESE GLASSES! We all fell silent for the most of the ride and cut to the airport. We met p there, bid our byes to their parents and were inside. All excited and ready to board the flight. We clicked pictures in the plane because why not. We were given a special pack of snacks because apparantly we had booked it online maybe(?) im not sure. But we felt special. We exited the flight and were met by our driver. Shady guy but acutally turned out to be a very helpful guide during our whole stay. If i were to name the filter for igram for this place then it would be ochre. The whole ride to our hotel was beautiful. We stopped by to click pictures w the sheeps, our driver made us taste this chai which was amazing. It was sunny but not hot. We reached hotel. We were given a deal by our driver for our safari. We thought of it all to be very shady at first but after talking to our dads, taking a leap of faith into trusting our driver after much scrutiny we said yes to him. After All he was also giving a good discount and he did try to prove himself to be quite trustworthy. Okay, enough about him. So we got all ready for our desert safari. After almost an hour's ride, we reached the sand dunes. It was beautiful. So bright and in expanse till your eyes can see. We then went for camel safari & jeep safari. We were now all claded in sand and our hair was a nest. But we still managed to look not all bad. Yes, I know, it's important to look pretty. All the fucking time. We were then told that there was a some kind of desert carnival and that kailash kher was going to show up. So we went ahead and found a cozy spot on one of the sand dunes. Now, the events that followed after werent we expected at all. In the span of an hour, we ate an icecream facing the moon, I lost my phone (found it later tg), we were met by few shady strangers, were catcalled, and were in desperate need to reach the hotel. It was a little too much for me personally, but in retrospect, it wasnt all that scary. So after that it was all fun and pretty. We ate dal batti. Kals tasted it for the first time and she loved it (tg)! We rode back to our hotel after an hour or two. K still had her paper for the psychology to write while I had my presentation for design thinking the next day. So we all bid our goodbyes and went to sleep early. In our cozy little beds, w cozy little lights on.

Day 2: Sunset Rollercoaster

I woke up around 8 for my presentation, which btw went awful but let's not get into that. K had to pull an all nighter for her exam. And P and Kals were basically having the time of their lives, looking all fine and dandy. After getting ready, we locked our rooms and went ahead looking forward for a bright day. We visited a lot of places. First we went to Jaisalmer's main attraction, Jaisalmer Fort. It's believed to be one of the very few living forts in ther world, as nearly one fourth of the city's population still resides within fort. The fort's yellow sandstone walls camouflages the fort in the yellow tawny desert. We then took a pit-stop at a juice centre before visiting Patwa Haveli and Nathmal Ki Haveli which is known for its intricate architecture & ornate carving with beautiful history. We then rushed to Bada Bagh for the sunset and it was the most beautiful sunset we had seen. Sun setting on the horizon with orange and red hues blanketing the sky complimenting the sandstone cenotaphs of sage Vyas. We also met a friendly dog who basically sat with us for the whole time. We watched the sunset while eating lays and surrounded by tranquility. We were done for the day and were famished. So our driver was happy to help us in suggesting restaurants and we diceded on a roadside one. While we placed an order, P got the news of her neighbour's dog. It was an emotional moment but there's nothing that the food cant fix. We gorged on the local delicacy called kel-sangri ki sabzi and roti. It was a beautiful time and we were ready to call it a day. After reaching the hotel, K ran to her room to finish her exam while we three sat near the pool, under the moonlight listening to the songs played by some other group. The moonlight reflecting off the pool's surface with atmospheric songs playing on the side, maybe a good way to end the day. After resting there for few hours, we bid our byes and hit the sack.

Day 3: Halcyon Day

The day of rest & recreation and getting drunk. The moment we reached the palace, our jaws dropped. Rajhasthan is sure known for its hopitality and amiable nature of its people but the royalty? no one can live like royals like them. This huge acre of land, made of sandstone and ornamented with Bougainvilleas, looked so beautiful. After a two hour drive from the main city, this view was worth it. Worth our driver's constant speculation for our choices. So firstly we divided ourselves and went into our respective rooms. K & P went straight into the pool while kals and me treated ourselves with the body massage. We went into our rooms and slept for an hour or two for we were on paradise. In the evening we got ready for the dinner where kals had a pretty rough time getting ready. After finally been done with all the grooming we went outside on the terrace to click pictures. The evening view of the palace took our breath away. We just stood their, admiring its beauty, before we turned back to our photo session. We then descended for dinner. There, we were met with the folk singers and a nice table waiting for us. We were served wine and other delicacies and joined the folk singers for a small jugalbandi and then dance. We loved the food, we loved their hospitality and we were drunk. We then retreated to our rooms, got into our cozy wear and danced madly in the room. We shouted, laughed, cried and sang and danced the night away. We then took our pillows and went to the terrace where we talked life and astrology. The moon was shining bright on us and we were in so much in love with the life at the moment. Everything fit perfectly well, we fit perfectly well. We hugged eachother and said our goodbyes.

Day 4: Bougainvillea Goodbyes

AAAAAAAAAAAh the last day! We slept around 4 so it was impossible for me to wake up in the morning for the palace tour. I met my friends at the breakfast. We had an amazing breakfast and just gorged on it for an hour or two before we went into our rooms to pack our bags. We clicked few photos and reached the airport around 11. We bid our byes to our driver who was great and talked about the best days of our life during our flight. It was indeed, our best days of our life. Missing it baaadly now. Until later.
